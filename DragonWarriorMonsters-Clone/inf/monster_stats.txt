This table should be more meaningful than those displaying only the [base stat]
under each attribute of a monster.


How to read this table:

1. Look up those monster growth tables displaying the [base stat] under each
   attribute.
2. Look for the [base stat] for the attribute of interest on the top row, and
   the monster's next level on the leftmost column.
3. The number at where the row and column intersect is the number of points that
   monster will gain in that attribute upon its next level up.
*. This table only works for [HP], [MP], [Attack], [Defense], [Agility] and
   [Intelligence]; it will not work for [EXP] or any other attributes listed on
   those generic tables.


Examples:

A monster with a [base stat] of 17 in [Attack] will gain 8 points in [Attack] at
level 45.

A monster with a [base stat] of 5 in [MP] will gain 4 [MP] at level 6.

Key:


Total: Total stat points gained by leveling up from 1 to 99.

Lv1:   Required stat points at level 1 in order to max out that attribute.

Max?:  0 means that attribute will never max out natually.
       1 means that attribute is able to maximize, provided it met its level 1
       stat requirement, and that the monster has a max level of 99.
       2 means that attribute will always max out at level 99 regardless of its
       stat at level 1.

(*)    Attributes with a [base stat] of 0 seem to randomly increase by 0 or 1 on
       level up, this is not confirmed.

(**)   Attributes with a [base stat] of 25 or higher will eventually max out no
       matter how low the stat at level 1 is, therefore the bottom rows of those
       columns might not be accurate.


****************
Notes:


1. Although generally speaking, that an attribute with a higher [base stat] is
   better, this is not always the case!

2. The stat increments in the table above is obtained without influences from
   high [pluses]. The [HP] and [Attack] attributes might gain more points due to
   high [pluses].

3. Certain [Rings] may affect the actual points gained upon level up.

4. This table only works for monsters hatched from [eggs], it is not tested on
   captured monsters. However, it should work the same after the level at which
   the monster is captured.

5. I will try to complete [base stats] 27 to 31 and tidy up the table A.S.A.P.


****************
Interesting findings:


1. The [base stats] 3, 6, 19 and 20 are good for your early game monsters only,
   because those attributes grow very fast at the beginning, but they will slow
   down after level 16.

2. Try to avoid the [base stat] 25 in early game, especially when your monsters
   don't have very high max levels, because its rapid growth only happens from
   level 42.

3. The [base stats] 9, 12, 15 and 18 are very good for your mid game monsters,
   because they grow steadily throughout the middle levels at a good pace.

4. For your late game monsters, look especially for those with [base stats] 12,
   18, 23 and 24, because they are very common and most are relatively easy to
   level up.

5. Try to avoid low-end monsters with the [base stat] 26 or 27 under [Attack] in
   early to mid game, because they generally have very low [MP],[Defense] and
   [Intelligence].

6. [Intelligence] is very important, because most ultimate spells require very
   high [Intelligence], and a monster with a very low [Intelligence] may miss a
   hit, or cast a wrong spell.

7. For your ultimate monsters, look for those with the most maximizable
   attributes, instead of those that grow rapidly at lower levels. I.e. it's
   better to have a monster with [base stats] 16, 12, 18, 25, 17, 11, than one
   with 19, 22, 20, 6, 27, 3. The latter may grow very fast in the beginning,
   but most of its attributes will never max out.
